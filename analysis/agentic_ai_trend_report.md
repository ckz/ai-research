## Executive Summary

The agentic AI market has exploded from a niche research topic to a $7.8B market in 2025, with projections reaching $52B by 2030. This report analyzes 160+ AI companies — 110 existing profiles plus 50 new agentic AI-focused profiles — to identify patterns, opportunities, and strategic insights for startups.

**Key findings:**
1. Agentic AI companies reach unicorn status 2-3x faster than previous AI waves
2. Vertical AI agents command 40-60% higher valuation multiples than horizontal platforms
3. The "AI wrapper" problem is real — but companies that solve workflow-level problems (not just prompt engineering) build lasting value
4. Voice AI is the most under-indexed category relative to its market potential
5. Open-source frameworks (CrewAI, LangChain) are becoming the rails of the ecosystem

## 3. Cross-Cutting Patterns

### 3.1 Fastest-Growing Companies Share These Traits

1. **Founder-market fit**: Harvey (ex-lawyer + ex-DeepMind), Cursor (MIT CS + AI), Sierra (Bret Taylor, ex-Salesforce co-CEO)
2. **Zero or near-zero marketing spend**: Cursor, Lovable, Bolt.new all grew organically
3. **Narrow initial focus**: One workflow, one persona, one industry → expand
4. **Multi-model architecture**: Best companies use multiple LLMs (OpenAI + Claude + Gemini) — not locked to one
5. **Built for where models are going**: Tackled complex problems that current models could barely handle, knowing future models would make them work better

**Pattern**: Big tech is acquiring, not building, in agentic AI. The acqui-hire/acquisition wave will intensify in 2026.

### 3.3 The Protocol Wars: MCP vs. A2A

Two standards are emerging for agent interoperability:
- **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** — Anthropic's standard for agents connecting to tools and APIs
- **A2A (Agent-to-Agent)** — Google's standard for cross-vendor agent communication

Companies building on both protocols have a hedging advantage. This mirrors the early web's protocol standardization — winners will be those who embrace open standards.

## 4. What Makes Agentic AI Products Work

### The 5 Success Factors

**1. Workflow Ownership, Not Feature Addition**
- Winning products own an entire workflow (Harvey owns legal research end-to-end)
- Losing products add AI features to existing workflows (features get commoditized)

**2. Trust Architecture**
- Harvey's 0.2% hallucination rate vs. 3-5% for generic LLMs
- Custom evaluation benchmarks (Harvey's BigLaw Bench, Cursor's repo-wide understanding)
- Compliance certifications (SOC 2, HIPAA) are table stakes, not differentiators

**3. Data Flywheel**
- The best companies get better with more usage (Hebbia improves with each financial analysis, EliseAI with each lease inquiry)
- This creates a compounding moat that pure technology cannot replicate

**4. Distribution Advantage**
- Platform companies (Vercel → v0, Salesforce → Agentforce) have built-in distribution
- Startups need to find other distribution edges: developer community (CrewAI), vertical expertise (Harvey), or viral product experience (Lovable, Bolt.new)

**5. Pricing Innovation**
- Per-seat pricing is giving way to per-agent, per-action, and outcome-based pricing
- Sierra charges based on successful resolutions, not seats
- This aligns vendor incentives with customer outcomes

2. **AI coding assistants (plugin-only)** — GitHub Copilot and Cursor dominate
3. **Horizontal "do-anything" agent platforms** — Without vertical focus, these struggle for PMF

## 6. Startup Playbook Insights

### What to Do
1. **Pick a regulated vertical** — Legal, healthcare, finance. Compliance is your moat.
2. **Start with one workflow** — Own it completely before expanding.
3. **Build for trust, not speed** — Low hallucination rates matter more than fast responses.
4. **Use multi-model architecture** — Don't bet on a single LLM provider.
5. **Price on outcomes** — Per-resolution, per-completed-task, not per-seat.
6. **Hire domain experts** — Harvey has lawyers on staff. Hippocratic has doctors. Domain knowledge > AI knowledge.

### What to Avoid
1. **Don't build a thin wrapper on top of OpenAI/Claude** — API access is not a moat.
2. **Don't compete with Cursor/Lovable in AI coding** — The category is settled.
3. **Don't raise too much too early** — Retell AI reached profitability on $4.6M. Capital efficiency signals real PMF.
4. **Don't ignore compliance** — SOC 2 and HIPAA aren't optional for enterprise sales.
5. **Don't lock into one LLM provider** — Model capabilities shift constantly.
