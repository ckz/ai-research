## Products & Features
- **Talent Intelligence Platform:** Deep learning AI that breaks down work into skills and transferable experiences, matching people to opportunities based on potential — not just job titles.
- **Talent Acquisition:** Helps recruiters find candidates with the potential to fill openings, even without a matching job title. AI-powered sourcing, screening, and matching.
- **Talent Management:** Matches internal employees to new opportunities, identifies skill gaps, and creates development pathways.
- **Workforce Planning:** Data-driven workforce analytics for strategic planning, project staffing, and future skills forecasting.
- **AI Interviewer (October 2025):** Automated AI-powered interviewing for faster and more equitable hiring processes.
- **Workforce Exchange:** Connects job seekers with visibility into how they fit across hundreds of openings.

## Competitive Position
Competing with: LinkedIn Talent Insights, Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, Oracle HCM, Phenom, Beamery, SeekOut, Google Cloud AI for HR.

**Key differentiator:** Deep learning AI purpose-built for talent intelligence, co-founded by Google/Facebook AI leaders with 80+ patents. The platform's ability to infer skills and potential (not just match keywords) is fundamentally different from traditional ATS/HCM approaches. The depth of founder AI expertise gives technical credibility that HR tech competitors lack.

## Strategic Notes
- Founded by genuine AI research leaders (Google, Facebook, IBM Research) with 80+ combined patents — this is not an AI wrapper, it is deep technical IP.
- $2.1B valuation from 2021 may be stale — the company has grown significantly since. Analysts project $3-4B, but a formal re-pricing has not occurred publicly.
- SoftBank Vision Fund 2 leading the Series E is a double-edged signal: massive capital access but also pressure for growth at all costs (the SoftBank playbook).
- S&P Global partnership (October 2025) for talent intelligence is a significant enterprise validation — S&P Global is a data and analytics powerhouse.
- The AI Interviewer launch signals expansion beyond matching and sourcing into the full hiring workflow.
- Risk: Algorithmic bias concerns persist in AI-driven hiring. Regulatory scrutiny (EU AI Act, NYC Local Law 144) could create compliance headwinds. Enterprise reluctance to share sensitive HR data with third-party cloud systems remains a barrier.
- Opportunity: Potential strategic alliances or acquisitions with SAP, Oracle, or global staffing conglomerates could accelerate growth dramatically.

## Status
Active — Leading AI talent intelligence platform with $2.1B+ valuation and 900+ employees. Expanding product suite with AI Interviewer and S&P Global partnership. Well-positioned in the growing AI-powered HR tech market.
