## Products & Features
- **AI Phone Agents** -- Hyper-realistic conversational AI agents that can make and receive phone calls at scale. Sound human, speak any language, and operate 24/7.
- **Enterprise Integration** -- Connects with existing CRMs, ERPs, and business systems. Agents can book appointments, update records, send texts, and trigger workflows within a single call.
- **Self-Hosted Infrastructure** -- Unlike competitors that rely on third-party APIs, Bland runs its own end-to-end voice AI infrastructure for ultra-low latency and reliability.
- **Custom Voice Cloning** -- Deploy agents with any voice profile.
- **Use Cases:** Customer support automation, outbound sales calls, appointment scheduling, insurance verification, lead qualification, and operations.
- **Key Differentiator:** Purpose-built enterprise voice AI platform with self-hosted infrastructure, versus competitors using stitched-together APIs.

## Competitive Position
Competing with: ElevenLabs ($3.3B valuation), Vapi, Retell AI, Bland competes with Cognigy, Parloa, Voiceflow, PolyAI, and custom solutions from Google CCAI, Amazon Connect, Twilio

Bland differentiates through enterprise focus, self-hosted low-latency infrastructure, and rapid deployment for high-volume phone operations.

## Strategic Notes
- The origin story (insurance denials during cancer treatment leading to Intelliga Health, then pivoting to general AI phone calls via Y Combinator) shows classic founder-problem-fit evolving into broader market opportunity.
- Pre-seed to Series B in 10 months is extraordinary fundraising velocity, signaling strong investor conviction in the voice AI market.
- Angel investors Max Levchin (PayPal) and Jeff Lawson (Twilio) are strategically relevant -- Lawson built the original telecom API platform, and his backing of Bland suggests he sees AI phone agents as the next evolution.
- Enterprise customers like Cleveland Cavaliers, University of Phoenix, and Better.com provide social proof but the customer list is still developing compared to more mature competitors.
- The voice AI market is projected at $45B+ and is still early. Bland's enterprise positioning and self-hosted infrastructure give it a structural advantage over API-stitching competitors.
- ElevenLabs ($3.3B valuation, $180M Series C) is the largest competitor in voice AI but focuses more broadly on voice synthesis; Bland is specifically focused on enterprise phone calls.
- The self-hosted infrastructure approach trades capital efficiency for latency advantage and data control -- important for enterprise customers with compliance requirements.

## Status
Active -- Fast-growing enterprise voice AI platform with exceptional fundraising velocity. YC-backed with strategic angel investors. Still early in revenue scaling but positioned in a large and rapidly expanding market.
