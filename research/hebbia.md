## Founders & Leadership
- **George Sivulka** -- Founder & CEO. From Staten Island, New York. Stanford prodigy: completed BS in Mathematics in under 3 years with Distinction (4.0 GPA), MS in Applied Physics as a Threshold Venture Fellow, and began a fully funded PhD in Electrical Engineering (machine learning and theoretical neuroscience) before dropping out at age 23 to found Hebbia. Previously worked at NASA. Pioneer in productionizing Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) -- credited as the first to bring RAG to production.

## Key Metrics
- **ARR:** ~$13M (June 2024, Sacra estimate); grew from ~$900K (Dec 2022) to ~$10M (Dec 2023) -- 15x growth in 18 months
- **Employees:** 140 (January 2026)
- **Market Penetration:** 33% of top 50 global asset managers by AUM are customers
- **Documents Processed:** 1 billion+ pages
- **Accuracy:** 92% with OpenAI o1 (vs. 68% with out-of-the-box RAG)
- **Gross Retention:** >90% among top asset managers, banks, and law firms
- **Pricing:** Professional seats $10,000/seat/year; Lite seats $3,000-$3,500/seat/year

## Products & Features
- **Matrix:** Flagship multi-agent AI platform that orchestrates multiple AI agents in parallel, leveraging OpenAI's o3-mini, o1, and GPT-4o simultaneously. Designed for the most complex financial and legal workflows end-to-end.
- **Document Analysis:** Extract, structure, and analyze millions of documents at scale using AI agents.
- **Mobile Application:** Launched late 2025, bringing document analysis to financial professionals on the go.
- **Enterprise Integrations:** PitchBook, Capital IQ, broker research, and other financial data sources.
- **Workflow Automation:** Users can build custom agents for specific workflows without technical training.

## Competitive Position
Competing with: Kira Systems, Eigen Technologies, Luminance, AlphaSense, Adept AI, Bloomberg Terminal (for research workflows).

Hebbia differentiates through Bloomberg-level pricing power, deep specialization in financial and legal workflows, and multi-agent orchestration that achieves 92% accuracy on complex document analysis tasks.

**Key Customers:** Centerview Partners, KKR, MetLife, Oak Hill Advisors, U.S. Air Force, and 33% of top 50 global asset managers.

**Customer Impact:**
- Investment bankers save 30-40 hours per deal on marketing materials and meeting prep.
- Private credit teams eliminate days of manual contract review.
- Private equity firms save 20-30 hours per deal on screening and due diligence.
- Law firms reduce credit agreement review time by 75%, saving $2,000/hour in legal fees.

## Strategic Notes
- Hebbia commands Bloomberg-level pricing ($10K/seat/year) -- rare for an AI startup, indicating strong value proposition in finance.
- 33% penetration of top 50 asset managers is remarkable for a startup founded in 2020 -- deep enterprise adoption in the most demanding vertical.
- 15x ARR growth in 18 months (through June 2024) with >90% gross retention signals product-market fit.
- The acquisition of FlashDocs (July 2025) adds document-to-draft generation, expanding from analysis to content creation.
- FactSet partnership (September 2025) and Microsoft Azure AI Foundry integration (August 2025) extend distribution.
- U.S. Air Force as a customer validates the platform beyond financial services.
- At $700M valuation (July 2024), Hebbia is likely due for an up-round given growth trajectory -- potential unicorn candidate.
- Key risk: Large AI labs (OpenAI, Anthropic) could build vertical-specific products that compete directly.

## Status
Active -- Series B company with strong financial services penetration, growing rapidly with 15x ARR growth and expanding into adjacent verticals (legal, government). Likely approaching unicorn valuation.
